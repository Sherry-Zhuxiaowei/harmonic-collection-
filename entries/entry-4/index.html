If you want to write about the suffering of women,
you cannot just write about the suffering of women.

You have to write about the baby girls who drowned in the river,

About the "chaste women" who, in anger, hit the stone wall.

In times of famine, women were sold,

And in prosperous times, they were bound by the concepts of "three obediences and four virtues."

The vibrant red wedding attire becomes a shackle,

The three-inch golden lotus feet are oppression.

Their rouge is like blood,

And they face the danger of encountering wolves at any moment.

Write about child brides,

About daughters forbidden from attending school,

About being confined within grand mansions where they cannot step out of the front door.

Write about bowing and scraping everywhere,

About constantly lowering their heads and being submissive. Washing clothes, and cooking meals,

They were slaves and maids.

"She cries, she resists, she feels utterly helpless."

It's about the words that echo in their ears,

Such as ”A burden that costs money,“

“Matchmaking,”

and “Eagerly awaiting a match,”

Casting a lifelong spell upon them like a curse.

Write about young girls forced to quit school to work,

In families with five or six sisters and only one brother.

Write about the relentless pressure to marry and bear children,

The anxiety over age,

Where neither the family nor the in-laws show any kindness.

Write about resumes discarded into trash bins,

About interviewers ruthlessly probing without boundaries.

Rumors and gossip all single her out,

Blaming her for everything,

"It's her who creates chaos, it's her who lacks knowledge, and it's her who bears all the blame."
