The Cage

In the cage of societal norms, she dwells,

A spirit once wild, now confined by their spells.

Her dreams, like captive birds, no longer soar,

As expectations and traditions she must explore.


They told her to be meek, to softly speak,

To follow their rules, to be mild and weak.

But beneath that mask, her fire burns bright,

A warrior's spirit, a fierce, untamed light.


She'll break those bars, shatter that cage,

Reclaim her essence, her passion, her rage.

For she's not a puppet, nor a porcelain doll,

She's a force of nature, breaking free from it all.


In the world's harsh spotlight, she'll proudly stand,

A testament to strength, a force to withstand.

No longer tamed, no longer confined,

She's the fierce, untamed spirit of womankind
